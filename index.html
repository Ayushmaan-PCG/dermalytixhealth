Innovation

Dermalytix shifts pressure-injury prevention from reactive checks to predictive, behavior-aware intelligence.

The Problem

Pressure injuries develop silently due to prolonged localized pressure. Manual checks and static thresholds fail to provide continuous awareness.

The Solution

  • Multi-point pressure sensing
  • Pressure-duration trend analysis
  • Actionable alerts designed for caregivers

Innovation Depth & Potential IP

Dermalytix introduces novel signal interpretation logic centered on pressure-duration behavior, redistribution patterns, and caregiver-action timing .

  • Multi-sensor trend aggregation
  • Adaptive risk scoring per patient
  • Workflow-aware alerts to reduce alarm fatigue

Based on early modeling and bench testing, Dermalytix is designed to reduce prolonged high-risk pressure exposure events by an estimated 30â€“50% in monitored care settings.

All IP references represent exploratory system-level concepts, not finalized clinical claims.

Technology

Hardware, firmware, and analytics operate as a unified system.

  • Thin-film FSR pressure sensors
  • ESP32-based edge processing and BLE streaming
  • Mobile app for visualization and alerts
  • Trend-based risk logic (rule-based + ML-assisted)

Sensor Mesh

Thin-film pressure sensors

Flexible FSR array placed under patient risk zones.

ESP32 Hub

On-bed edge computing

Reads sensors, preprocesses signals, and streams BLE data.

AI Risk Engine

Predictive analytics

Analyzes pressure magnitude, duration, micro-shift variability, and cumulative load to predict early tissue ischemia risk â€” a precursor to pressure injury formation.

Adaptive Calibration

Personalized thresholds

Tailors risk scoring to mattress, body type, and mobility.

Data & Validation

Experimental prototyping

Sensor data is collected across controlled pressure scenarios, processed into feature vectors, and evaluated using rule-based and ML-assisted risk models. System accuracy improves with adaptive calibration over time.

Validation & Practicality

Prototype feasibility validated through structured bench testing.

  • Consistent detection of threshold crossings
  • Stable readings under static load
  • Clear differentiation between transient and sustained pressure
